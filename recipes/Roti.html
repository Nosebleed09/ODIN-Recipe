Roti

Description

Roti is a staple Indian flatbread made from whole wheat flour, water, and sometimes a bit of oil or ghee. Itâ€™s rolled out into thin discs and cooked on a hot tawa or pan until lightly charred and puffed. It's commonly served with curries, vegetables, and other dishes.

Ingredients for the dish

  • 1 cup whole wheat flour (atta)
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt (optional)
  • 1/2 cup water (adjust as needed)
  • 1 teaspoon oil or ghee (optional, for kneading)

Lets cook it !

  1. Mix the flour and salt: In a large bowl, combine the whole wheat flour and salt (if using).
  2. Add water gradually: Slowly add water to the flour, mixing it with your fingers to form a dough. Start with a little water and add more as needed. The dough should be soft but not sticky.
  3. Knead the dough: Knead the dough for about 5-7 minutes until smooth and pliable. If the dough is too sticky, add a little more flour; if it's too dry, add a bit more water. You can also add a teaspoon of oil or ghee while kneading for extra softness.
  4. Rest the dough: Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.
  5. Divide and roll: After resting, divide the dough into small, equal-sized balls (about the size of a golf ball). Flatten a ball slightly, dust it with flour, and roll it out into a thin, round disc (about 6-7 inches in diameter).
  6. Cook the roti:
    • Heat a tawa or flat pan on medium heat. Once hot, place the rolled-out roti on the pan.
    • Cook for about 30 seconds or until you see bubbles forming. Flip the roti.
    • Cook the other side for another 30 seconds until light brown spots appear.
    • Press gently with a cloth or spatula, allowing the roti to puff up.
  7. Optional step: If you prefer, after cooking both sides, you can place the roti directly on an open flame using tongs to let it puff up fully. Quickly remove from the flame.
  8. Serve: Brush the cooked roti with ghee or butter if desired, and serve hot with your favorite curry or side dish.
