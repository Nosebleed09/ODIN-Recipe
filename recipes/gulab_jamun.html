Gulaab Jamun

Description

Gulab Jamun is a classic Indian sweet made from deep-fried khoya (milk solids) balls soaked in a fragrant sugar syrup. The balls are cooked until golden brown and then soaked in a warm syrup flavored with cardamom and rose water, creating a soft, syrupy dessert.

Ingredients for the dish

  • 1 cup khoya (mawa), crumbled
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 2 tablespoons milk (adjust as needed)
  • Ghee or oil for deep frying

Lets cook it !

Prepare the Sugar Syrup:

  1. Make the syrup: In a large pan, combine sugar and water. Heat the mixture until the sugar dissolves completely.
  2. Flavor the syrup: Add the crushed cardamom pods and saffron strands (if using). Let the syrup simmer on low heat for 5-7 minutes until it thickens slightly.
  3. Add rose water: Turn off the heat and stir in the rose water or essence. Keep the syrup warm.

Prepare the Gulab Jamun:

  1. Make the dough: In a mixing bowl, combine crumbled khoya, all-purpose flour, and baking powder. Mix well.
  2. Add milk: Gradually add milk, a little at a time, and gently knead the mixture into a soft, smooth dough. Be careful not to over-knead.
  3. Shape the dough: Divide the dough into small, equal-sized balls (about the size of a marble). Ensure there are no cracks on the surface to avoid the gulab jamun breaking during frying.
  4. Heat the oil: Heat ghee or oil in a deep pan over low to medium heat. The oil should be moderately hot but not too hot, as the gulab jamun need to cook slowly and evenly.
  5. Fry the gulab jamun: Gently drop the balls into the oil and fry them on low heat, stirring occasionally, until they turn golden brown. This process may take 6-8 minutes.
  6. Soak in syrup: Once fried, remove the gulab jamun from the oil and immediately place them in the warm sugar syrup. Let them soak for at least 1-2 hours to absorb the syrup fully.
